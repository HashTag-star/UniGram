## Brand Essence

> **"Your campus, your voice, your world."**

UniGram exists for one reason: to give African university students a space that was made for them — not adapted for them, not reluctantly tolerated by them, but built from the ground up with their lives, culture, and campuses in mind. Every pixel, every word, every interaction should feel like it came from a student who gets it. Because it did.

---

## Brand Story

There are over 200,000 university students in Ghana. They live on WhatsApp groups that are too noisy. They perform on Instagram for an audience that includes their parents. They argue on Twitter alongside people who have never set foot on a university campus. They buy and sell in Facebook Marketplace among strangers.

UniGram says: enough. Your campus deserves its own network. Your voice deserves to be heard by the people who actually share your world — the person in your lecture hall, the coursemate who copies your notes, the crush you've been avoiding in the Science Library.

UniGram is not just an app. It is infrastructure for student life.

---

## Brand Personality

UniGram is a person, not a platform. If UniGram were a student, they would be:
- That person in your department who knows everyone, has been to every event, and always has something interesting to say
- Smart but never arrogant — they earned their grades but they'll still help you pass
- Ghanaian to the core but Pan-African in their vision — they can switch from Twi to Pidgin to French without blinking
- Funny in a way that doesn't try too hard — they land jokes because they're actually observant, not because they rehearsed
- Deeply loyal to their campus but not in a small-minded way — they celebrate KNUST while also respecting what Legon brings

## Tone of Voice

**The UniGram voice is: Bold. Real. Collegiate. Pan-African. Witty.**

### Bold
We speak with conviction. We don't hedge. We don't say "we think UniGram might be useful for students." We say "UniGram is built for you, and there's nothing like it."

### Real
We talk like a student, not a press release. We use contractions. We use informal punctuation when it suits the post. We use "us" and "we" — because UniGram is the students using it. Never use the word "leverage" in a public post. Never say "solutions." Never say "ecosystem" unless you're a biologist.

### Collegiate
We understand campus life from the inside. We reference the real stuff: exam stress, hostel drama, the canteen queue, the final-year pressure, the internship hustle, departmental clashes, SRC elections, end-of-semester celebrations.

### Pan-African
We are Ghanaian first, but we are proudly connected to the wider African student experience. We know UNILAG, we know Nairobi, we know Stellenbosch. We use Afrobeats references, we celebrate African excellence, we do not look to the West for validation.

### Witty
We make people smile. We are not corporate-funny (forced, hollow). We are campus-funny — observational, sharp, self-aware. We can be sarcastic but we are never mean.

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|

| Success Green | #38a169 | Positive confirmations, completed actions |
| Mid-grey | #a0aec0 | Secondary text, placeholder content, disabled states |

### Colour Rules
- Never place Deep Indigo text on Electric Blue background (insufficient contrast)
- Never use white (#ffffff) — always use Off-white (#f0f0f8)
- The app background (#05050a) should never be replaced with pure black (#000000)
- Deep Indigo and Electric Blue should not appear in equal visual weight in the same element — one must dominate

## Typography

### Primary Font: Outfit
Outfit is UniGram's sole typeface. It ships with the app and must be used in all branded materials where possible.

| Weight | Use |
|---|---|
| Outfit Bold (700) | Headlines, app feature names, poster headlines, social media text overlays |
| Outfit SemiBold (600) | Subheadings, card titles, button labels |
| Outfit Regular (400) | Body text, captions, descriptions, paragraph copy |
| Outfit Light (300) | Timestamps, metadata, fine print (use sparingly) |

### Type Scale

| Element | Size | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline (poster/banner) | 48–60pt | Bold |
| Section headline | 32–40pt | Bold |
| Card/post title | 20–24pt | SemiBold |
| Body paragraph | 14–16pt | Regular |
| Caption / metadata | 11–12pt | Light or Regular |

### Type Rules
- Headlines are sentence case (not ALLCAPS or Title Case), unless using 2–3 word slogans
- No serif fonts ever — Outfit only
- Minimum body text size: 14pt (accessibility)
- Never stretch or condense the Outfit font

## Logo Guidelines

### The UniGram Wordmark
The UniGram logo is a wordmark: the name "UniGram" set in Outfit Bold with a custom ligature treatment on the "U" and "G." The wordmark is the primary brand asset.

**Clear space rule:** Always maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the capital "U" around all sides of the wordmark. Nothing — no text, no other logos, no graphic elements — enters this space.

**Approved logo versions:**
1. Off-white wordmark on Deep Indigo background (primary)
2. Off-white wordmark on Near-black background (dark contexts)
3. Deep Indigo wordmark on Off-white background (light contexts, print)
4. Electric Blue wordmark on Near-black background (accent/special use only)

**Minimum size:** The wordmark should not be reproduced at less than 80px wide in digital contexts or 20mm wide in print.

### What You Must Never Do to the Logo
- Do not change any letter colour individually
- Do not apply a drop shadow
- Do not place the wordmark on a busy photographic background without a solid colour backing
- Do not rotate, skew, or distort
- Do not add a tagline directly to the wordmark (taglines are set separately, below, in smaller type)
- Do not use the wordmark in a colour that is not in the approved palette

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

### 10 Things We Always Do

1. **Speak directly to the student.** "You" is the most important word in our vocabulary.
2. **Celebrate campus specificity.** Name the campus. Name the hall. Name the struggle. KNUST students know the Brunei Hostel queue. Legon students know the Commonwealth Hall noise. Use it.
3. **Post in the morning and in the evening.** Students check their phones after waking up and after evening lectures. Be there.
4. **Use real student photography.** Real faces, real campus locations, real uniform-free moments. Authenticity is everything.
5. **Laugh at ourselves occasionally.** If the app has a quirk or a known issue, acknowledge it with humour before users do.
6. **Credit and celebrate student creators.** When a student's post goes viral on UniGram, amplify it. Their win is our win.
7. **Mix English with local language naturally.** "Ei, this app be different walai." It should feel like something a real student typed.
8. **Keep video content under 60 seconds.** Attention is scarce. Make every second land.
9. **Use Ghanaian cultural references.** "That KNUST exam wahala," "the Legon Library heat," "hostel water situation" — these create immediate recognition.
10. **Engage comments within 4 hours.** Every response is an opportunity to show UniGram is human.

### 10 Things We Never Do

1. **Never use stock photography.** No smiling people in suits. No generic "African students" from Unsplash. Never.
2. **Never be preachy.** We don't tell students what to think about social issues. We provide the space; they lead the conversation.
3. **Never use corporate jargon.** "Synergy," "leverage," "ecosystem," "solutions," "value proposition" — banned from all public-facing copy.
4. **Never ignore a complaint publicly.** If a student calls us out publicly, we respond with empathy and action, not deflection.
5. **Never mock a rival university.** Inter-campus banter is fun; institutional mockery is not. We celebrate all campuses.
6. **Never post without proofreading.** One typo in a headline damages credibility. Two typos and we become a meme (and not the good kind).
7. **Never use excessive emojis.** One or two emojis per post maximum. We are not a food delivery service.
8. **Never announce features before they're ready.** Overpromising kills trust with students faster than with any other demographic.
9. **Never repost without attribution.** Student content is credited. Always. Full stop.
10. **Never go silent during a controversy.** If something goes wrong, the worst thing we can do is disappear. Address it fast, address it honestly.

## Photography and Content Style

### The UniGram Aesthetic
UniGram content should look like it was taken by a student on a good phone — not shot by a professional crew on a $10,000 camera. High production value is fine; the *feeling* of over-production is not.

**Shot styles that work:**
- Students in natural campus environments: libraries, lecture theatres (after class, not during), canteens, hostel corridors, sports fields, under trees
- Candid moments of genuine laughter, genuine stress, genuine celebration
- Close-ups of student work, notebooks, laptop screens with relevant content
- Event moments: concerts, SRC elections, graduation ceremonies, sport days
- Food — because Ghanaian campus food culture is a whole personality

**Shot styles that do not work:**
- People looking directly at the camera with a forced smile in a blank background
- Staged "studying" photos where no one is actually studying
- Anything that looks like it came from a corporate CSR report
- Photos where students are in formal wear (unless it's a graduation post)

**Colour treatment:**
- Warm, slightly saturated tones that feel alive — not over-filtered, not desaturated
- Dark-toned content for app feature cards (matching the Near-black app background)
- Accent Electric Blue (#6366f1) used sparingly as a graphic overlay element

**Video content:**
- Handheld or phone footage is acceptable and often preferred (feels real)
- Always subtitle video content (accessibility + students watch with sound off)
- Music should be Afrobeats, Highlife, Amapiano, or Afropop — never generic royalty-free EDM

## Brand Application Checklist

Before any piece of content or design goes public, verify:

- [ ] Uses only Outfit font
- [ ] Uses only approved colour palette
- [ ] Photography is real students, not stock
- [ ] Copy has been read aloud — does it sound like a human wrote it?
- [ ] No corporate jargon
- [ ] Clear space respected if logo appears
- [ ] Campus-specific if targeting a specific campus
- [ ] Subtitled if video content
- [ ] Social captions reviewed by at least one team member before posting
