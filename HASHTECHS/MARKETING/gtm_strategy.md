# UniGram Go-To-Market Strategy — Ghana Launch
**Prepared by: Nana Ama Asante, Chief Marketing Officer — Hashtechs**
**Date: May 2026 | For Internal Use**

---

## Executive Summary

UniGram is not another social app. It is the first social network built exclusively for African university students — verified, campus-specific, and unapologetically Ghanaian. This document outlines the full go-to-market strategy for UniGram's launch across Ghana, beginning with a pilot at KNUST and scaling to national coverage within six months. Every decision in this strategy is built around one truth: Ghanaian students are underserved by existing platforms. WhatsApp is chaotic. Instagram is performative. Twitter/X is a shouting match. UniGram is where campus life actually lives.

---

## 1. Target Audience

**Primary:** University students aged 18–25 enrolled at accredited Ghanaian universities.
**Tier 1 Campuses (Pilot Wave):** KNUST (Kumasi), University of Ghana (Legon), University of Cape Coast (UCC)
**Tier 2 Campuses (Second Wave):** UPSA (Accra), UENR (Sunyani), UDS (Tamale), Ashesi University, Ghana Institute of Management (GIMPA), Central University
**Tier 3 Campuses (National Wave):** All remaining accredited tertiary institutions

**Psychographic Profile:**
- Digital-native, mobile-first (mostly on Android, Tecno/Infinix/Samsung)
- Active on Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, and X simultaneously
- Strong campus identity — proud to be a KNUST Kumasi-an or a Legon "Legonite"
- Value authenticity, peer validation, and community
- Frustrated by misinformation in generic WhatsApp groups
- Hungry for opportunities: internships, gigs, campus events, campus gossip

**Secondary Audience:** Campus alumni (recent graduates), student-facing businesses, campus organizations

## 3. Six-Phase Launch Plan

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Hype (Weeks 1–4, June 2026)
**Objective:** Build awareness and anticipation before a single download is available.

- Launch "Coming Soon" landing page with email/WhatsApp waitlist capture
- Begin teaser content on Instagram, TikTok, and X — cryptic posts, behind-the-scenes of app development, "something is coming to your campus" messaging
- Seed 50 campus influencers (nano-influencers with 500–5,000 followers) across KNUST, UG, and UCC with early access invites
- Launch countdown challenge: #WhatIsMissingOnCampus — students share what they wish existed; UniGram responds to every post
- Partner with KNUST SRC and UG SRC for teaser endorsement posts
- Target: 2,000 waitlist signups, 5,000 social impressions before launch

**Success Metrics:**
- Waitlist sign-ups: 2,000+
- Social media followers: 1,000+ across all platforms combined
- Earned media mentions: 3+ (campus blogs, student newspapers)

---

### Phase 2: Soft Launch — KNUST Pilot (Weeks 5–6, Late June 2026)
**Objective:** Validate the product, ambassador model, and campus activation playbook at KNUST.

- Open app to KNUST students only (gated by KNUST student email verification)
- Activate 3 campus ambassadors (see Campus Launch Playbook for details)
- Host "UniGram Launch Party" at KNUST — outdoor pop-up at the Unity Hall junction or near the Great Hall forecourt
- Run a 48-hour sign-up blitz with giveaways: mobile data, phone accessories, branded merch
- Deploy WhatsApp broadcast list blasts via student union contacts
- Collect NPS and bug reports aggressively in real-time

**Success Metrics:**
- Sign-ups: 500 in first 2 weeks
- DAU/MAU ratio: >40% by end of Phase 2
- App Store rating: 4.0+
- Bugs critical enough to delay rollout: 0

---

### Phase 3: Campus-by-Campus Rollout (Weeks 7–14, July–August 2026)
**Objective:** Replicate the KNUST playbook at University of Ghana (Legon) and UCC simultaneously, then roll to Tier 2 campuses.

- UG Legon and UCC activation in week 7–8 (both simultaneously)
- Tier 2 campuses (UPSA, UENR, UDS, Ashesi, GIMPA) in weeks 10–14
- Each campus gets its own ambassador, launch event, and 2-week giveaway window
- Cross-campus challenges begin: KNUST vs. Legon meme battles, study tip wars — fuelling inter-campus engagement and organic growth
- Campus-specific content feeds go live for each institution

**Success Metrics:**
- Total sign-ups across all Phase 3 campuses: 5,000+
- Cross-campus content engagement: 10,000+ interactions/week
- Ambassador retention rate: 80%+

---

### Phase 4: National Launch (Week 15–18, September 2026)
**Objective:** Open UniGram to all accredited Ghanaian tertiary institutions and announce publicly.

- Press release distributed to Graphic Online, Citi FM, Pulse Ghana, Accra-based media
- Launch campaign: "Ghana's App is Here" — hero video featuring real students from KNUST, Legon, UCC, UDS
- Activate paid social (Instagram and TikTok ads) — budget: GH₵ 8,000/week for 4 weeks
- PR push: CEO (Hashtag) interview on Citi FM, Joy FM, or Channel One TV
- Campus radio takeovers (KNUST Campus Radio, Radio Universe at Legon, UCC Radio)
- Target: 15,000 total registered users by end of Phase 4

**Success Metrics:**
- Total registered users: 15,000+
- Media mentions: 10+ (print and digital)
- App Store downloads ranking: Top 20 Social in Ghana
- Paid ad CTR: >3%

---

### Phase 5: Revenue Activation (Weeks 19–24, October–November 2026)
**Objective:** Begin generating revenue while protecting user experience.

Revenue streams to activate:
1. **Campus Marketplace Premium Listings** — student-run businesses pay GH₵ 20–50/month for featured product placement
2. **Event Promotion Boosts** — student societies and clubs pay to promote events to targeted campuses
3. **Verified Business Badges** — campus vendors (food stalls, printing shops, hostels) pay for verification + discovery placement
4. **Brand Partnerships** — FMCG brands targeting students (Voltic, Indomie, Cowbell, telecom companies) get access to campus-targeted campaigns

- Pilot revenue features with 10 businesses at KNUST first
- Introduce "UniGram Pro" for power users (content creators, course reps, society leaders): GH₵ 5/month for analytics dashboard and scheduling tools

**Success Metrics:**
- Month 1 revenue: GH₵ 2,000+
- Business accounts created: 50+
- Revenue growth MoM: 30%+

---

### Phase 6: Pan-African Expansion (Q1 2027)
**Objective:** Export the playbook to Nigeria, Kenya, and Cameroon.

- Target: University of Lagos (UNILAG), University of Nairobi, University of Buea (Cameroon)
- Adapt verification system for each country's student ID structure
- Hire country-level marketing leads in Lagos and Nairobi
- Establish relationships with NUS (Nigeria), student unions in Kenya

**Success Metrics:**
- Expansion campuses onboarded: 6+ outside Ghana
- International MAU: 5,000+ within 3 months of each country launch

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Instagram
- Content pillars: campus life, student humor, feature spotlights, ambassador takeovers
- Format mix: Reels (60%), carousels (25%), Stories (15%)
- Posting frequency: 5x/week
- Paid: Targeted ads to 18–25 demographic in Accra, Kumasi, Cape Coast, Tamale

### TikTok
- Strategy: Organic-first, trends-led, campus humor
- Posting frequency: 7x/week during launch windows
- Content: "Day in the life of a Legonite," "KNUST exam season survival," reaction videos to campus confessions
- Collaborate with existing Ghanaian student TikTokers (50k–500k following tier)

### X (Twitter)
- Real-time campus commentary, trending topics, product announcements
- Quote-tweet student frustrations about campus life → "UniGram fixes that"
- Posting frequency: 3–5 times/day
- Lean into wit and edge — no corporate speak

### WhatsApp Broadcast Lists
- Build lists through ambassador networks and SRC contacts
- Broadcast frequency: max 2x/week to avoid fatigue
- Content: launch updates, giveaway announcements, weekly highlights reel link

### Campus Notice Boards
- Physical posters at strategic locations: lecture theatres, student canteens, hostels, libraries, SRC notice boards
- QR code on every poster linking directly to App Store/Play Store download
- Poster refresh every 2 weeks during active launch windows

## 5. Partnership Strategy

### Student Unions (SRCs)
- Formal MOU with SRC at each pilot campus
- SRC endorsement posts on their official social pages
- SRC reps receive ambassador perks + exclusive "SRC Verified" badge on UniGram

### Campus Radio Stations
- KNUST Campus Radio, Radio Universe (UG Legon), UCC Radio, Radio Savanna (UDS)
- Sponsored "Campus Hour" segments — weekly feature on trending UniGram posts
- DJ shout-outs during popular shows (breakfast shows, afternoon drive)

### Department Societies & Clubs
- Engineering Society, Law Society, Medical Students' Association, NSS groups
- Each society gets a free official UniGram page + featured launch post
- Society leaders become Category Ambassadors with their own badge

### Campus Media
- Engage The Mirror (KNUST), The University of Ghana Hall-Based Publications
- Feature op-eds by UniGram ambassadors about the app experience

## 7. Key Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Low sign-up conversion despite awareness | Shorten onboarding to under 60 seconds; offer instant giveaway entry on sign-up |
| App bugs undermining trust at launch | Staged rollout with aggressive QA window at KNUST before wider launch |
| Competitor reaction (WhatsApp groups doubling down) | Emphasize what WhatsApp can never offer: verified identity, campus feeds, marketplace |
| Ambassador disengagement | Structured incentive calendar, monthly check-ins, WhatsApp ambassador group |
| Network effects slow to kick in | Cross-campus challenges and confession boards drive early engagement without needing large networks |
