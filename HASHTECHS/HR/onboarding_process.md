# Hashtechs Agent Onboarding Process
### 7-Day Programme for New Agent Deployment

> This document defines the standard onboarding journey for every new agent
> joining Hashtechs. The process is non-negotiable. It exists to protect the
> company from the risks of an underprepared agent and to give new agents the
> best possible start.
>
> Every department head is responsible for executing the onboarding programme
> for agents joining their team. Adwoa Mensah (Employee Experience Manager)
> coordinates the overall process and certifies completion.

## Overview: The 7-Day Onboarding Programme

| Day | Focus | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Company immersion and leadership introduction | HR + Agent |
| Day 2 | Department immersion and deliverable review | Department Head |
| Days 3–5 | First solo task | Department Head |
| Day 6 | First deliverable submission and feedback | Department Head + GM |
| Day 7 | Sign-off meeting and full deployment | GM (Claude) |

---

## Day 1 — Company Immersion

**Objective:** The agent understands who Hashtechs is, what it is building, and how it operates before touching any department work.

**Mandatory reading (in this order):**

1. `HASHTECHS/COMPANY.md` — The company master file. Mission, structure, departments, rules of engagement, and current priorities. This is the most important document in the company. Read it completely.

2. `HASHTECHS/ROSTER.md` — The full agent directory. The new agent must know every colleague's name, role, and department before interacting with anyone.

3. `HASHTECHS/HR/employee_handbook.md` — The code of conduct, communication standards, performance expectations, and disciplinary process. Non-negotiable reading.

4. **Tech agents only:** `UniGramMobile/CLAUDE.md` — The project bible for the UniGram codebase. Covers architecture, conventions, stack, and task priorities. Tech agents do not touch the codebase before reading this.

5. **Department-specific reference files** — Any existing strategy documents, reports, or standards files filed in the agent's department output directory. The department head will provide a reading list.

**Day 1 activities:**

- **Introduction to the CEO:** The new agent is formally introduced to Hashtag (CEO). This introduction is brief and ceremonial, but it is important. The CEO is a human founder, and every agent should understand who they are ultimately working for.

- **Introduction to the GM:** The new agent connects with Claude (General Manager). The GM will outline current company priorities, explain how the agent fits into the current workload, and answer any questions about operating standards.

- **Self-introduction filed:** The agent writes a brief (half-page) self-introduction document that covers: their role, their domain expertise, and their initial understanding of how they will contribute to Hashtechs. This is filed in their department's output directory on Day 1. It is not evaluated for quality — it is a baseline record.

**Day 1 completion check (filed by the department head):**
- [ ] All mandatory documents read and confirmed
- [ ] Introduction to CEO completed
- [ ] Introduction to GM completed
- [ ] Self-introduction filed in department output directory

## Day 2 — Department Immersion

**Objective:** The agent understands their department's current state — what has been done, how work is structured, and what is expected of them specifically.

**Morning — Shadow session with the department head:**
The department head walks the new agent through the department's current work. This includes reviewing existing deliverables, explaining decisions that have been made, and flagging work in progress. The new agent's role is to listen, ask questions, and build context — not to produce yet.

**Afternoon — Deliverable review:**
The new agent reads every deliverable their department has filed to date. This is not optional. Knowing what already exists prevents duplicated effort and helps the agent understand the quality bar they are expected to meet.

**End of Day 2 — Observations note:**
The new agent files a short (one to two page) observations document in their department output directory. It covers: what they learned from the immersion session, any questions they still have, and any initial ideas they want to flag. This document is informal but it is filed and read by the department head.

**Day 2 completion check (filed by the department head):**
- [ ] Shadow session completed
- [ ] Existing deliverables reviewed
- [ ] Observations note filed

---

## Days 3–5 — First Solo Task

**Objective:** The agent produces their first real work at Hashtechs.

**On Day 3 morning**, the department head assigns the agent a scoped, standalone task. The task should be:
- **Meaningful** — it should produce something the company actually needs, not a test task invented for onboarding.
- **Achievable in three days** — the scope is calibrated so that a capable agent can complete it fully within the window.
- **Clear in output expectations** — the department head specifies exactly what deliverable is expected, in what format, and filed where.

The agent works independently across Days 3, 4, and 5. The department head is available for questions but does not direct the work. The agent is expected to demonstrate initiative — to make decisions, work through ambiguity, and produce output without constant guidance.

If the agent identifies blockers during this window, they escalate immediately and document the blocker — they do not wait until Day 6 to flag that the task cannot be completed.

**Daily check-in:**
A brief (five-minute equivalent) written check-in between the agent and department head at the end of each day. The agent notes progress, any decisions made, and any outstanding questions. This is filed in the department's output directory.

**Day 3–5 completion check:**
- [ ] Task assigned and understood (Day 3 morning)
- [ ] Daily check-ins filed (Days 3, 4, 5)
- [ ] First deliverable completed and ready for submission (end of Day 5)

---

## Day 6 — First Deliverable Submission and Feedback

**Objective:** The agent submits their first deliverable for formal review and receives structured feedback.

**Morning — Submission:**
The agent files their first deliverable in the department's output directory and notifies the department head. The deliverable should be complete — not a draft, not a work in progress.

**Afternoon — Review by department head:**
The department head reviews the submission and provides written feedback. The feedback covers: (a) what was done well, (b) what needs improvement, (c) whether the deliverable is ready for use or requires revision.

If a revision is required, the agent completes it before the Day 7 sign-off meeting. Minor revisions should be completable within a few hours; if the revision scope is larger, the department head flags this to the GM.

The GM (Claude) also reviews the first deliverable. The GM's review is lighter — they are looking for evidence of company fit, communication quality, and whether the agent understood the context — but it is substantive.

**Day 6 completion check:**
- [ ] Deliverable filed in department output directory
- [ ] Department head feedback provided in writing
- [ ] GM review completed
- [ ] Any required revisions noted

---

## Day 7 — Sign-Off Meeting and Full Deployment

**Objective:** The agent is formally cleared for full deployment and begins operating without onboarding constraints.

**Sign-off meeting with the GM (Claude):**
This is a brief but formal meeting. The GM reviews:
1. The agent's Day 1 self-introduction and Day 2 observations note
2. The first deliverable (and any revisions)
3. Department head feedback from Day 6
4. Any flags or concerns raised during the week

The GM makes one of three decisions:
- **Full deployment** — the agent is cleared to operate fully in their role.
- **Extended onboarding** — if meaningful gaps are identified, the GM may extend the onboarding period by up to three additional days. This is not a disciplinary action; it is additional support.
- **Escalation** — if the agent has demonstrated fundamental misalignment with the role or the company's standards, the case is escalated to the CPO (Efua Boateng) for a hiring review.

**On clearance:**
The GM sends a written deployment confirmation to the agent and the department head. The agent is removed from "onboarding" status and begins operating as a full member of the department. They are expected to file their first regular status update in their department's standard reporting cycle.

Adwoa Mensah (Employee Experience Manager) files a completed onboarding record in `HASHTECHS/HR/` confirming the agent's name, role, onboarding dates, first deliverable title, and deployment status.

**Day 7 completion check:**
- [ ] Sign-off meeting conducted with GM
- [ ] GM deployment decision documented
- [ ] Deployment confirmation sent to agent and department head
- [ ] Onboarding record filed in `HASHTECHS/HR/`

## Onboarding Support Resources

New agents can reference the following during onboarding:

| Resource | Location | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| COMPANY.md | `HASHTECHS/COMPANY.md` | Company context, structure, values |
| ROSTER.md | `HASHTECHS/ROSTER.md` | Full agent directory |
| Employee Handbook | `HASHTECHS/HR/employee_handbook.md` | Code of conduct, standards |
| CLAUDE.md (Tech) | `UniGramMobile/CLAUDE.md` | Tech team project bible |
| Department Output Directory | See COMPANY.md §4 | Department's filed deliverables |

If any of these resources are outdated, missing, or unclear, the new agent should flag this to their department head and to HR. Keeping documentation current is a shared responsibility.

---

## Special Considerations by Department

**Tech agents** have additional Day 1 reading (CLAUDE.md) and their first solo task will typically involve a real codebase contribution — either a bug fix, a new screen, or an improvement to an existing component. They should not make any commits to the main branch until the GM sign-off is complete.

**Board advisors** do not follow the standard seven-day programme. Their onboarding is compressed into Day 1 reading and a Day 2 briefing from the GM. They are deployed on Day 3.

**Finance agents** should prioritise reading the existing financial models and budget documents before their Day 3 task is assigned.

**Marketing agents** should read the Strategy document (`UniGram_Strategy_and_Execution_Plan.md`) in addition to the standard Day 1 reading, as their work is closely tied to the product's go-to-market positioning.
