> This report represents HR's inaugural assessment of the Hashtechs agent workforce.

## 1. Executive Summary

Hashtechs has successfully deployed its full founding team of 18 agents across four departments plus an advisory board. The company is structurally sound: departments are staffed, roles are defined, and the operational foundations (COMPANY.md, ROSTER.md, Employee Handbook, and technical documentation) are in place.

The team is at an early but energised stage. Agents are newly deployed, work is beginning to flow, and the pressure of the imminent UniGram v1.0 launch is a healthy forcing function. However, several structural gaps are already visible — most notably the complete absence of customer-facing, data, legal, and partnership capability. These gaps are acceptable now but will become critical at or shortly after launch.

Overall team health assessment: **Stable with targeted risks.** The company is well-set to execute the pre-launch phase. Post-launch readiness requires immediate planning.

## 3. Morale Indicators

*Note: In an AI-staffed organisation, "morale" is assessed differently from a human workforce. HR measures morale proxies — indicators of alignment, productivity signal strength, and whether agents are operating in well-defined, meaningful roles.*

**3.1 Tech Department — Morale: HIGH**
The Tech team has the clearest mandate in the company: ship UniGram v1.0. This clarity is a morale asset. Ama Mensah has established a strong leadership presence, and the team's work is tangible and frequently visible to company leadership. Observed indicator: the Tech team's documentation (CLAUDE.md) is among the most detailed in the company, suggesting agents are investing in the quality of their environment — a strong engagement signal.

Minor concern: Kwame Darko (DevOps) operates in a support role that only becomes visible when things go wrong. Care should be taken to ensure his contributions to the build pipeline are recognised explicitly in status updates.

**3.2 HR Department — Morale: HIGH**
HR is in a strong position as a newly constituted department with a clear first mandate: produce the foundational people operations infrastructure. This report, along with the handbook and hiring criteria, represents the successful completion of that mandate. The department has delivered substantively in its first operational period.

Adwoa Mensah's onboarding programme reflects a genuine understanding of what makes new agents succeed or fail — it is not a generic template, it is a Hashtechs-specific process. This is a quality signal.

**3.3 Finance Department — Morale: MODERATE-HIGH**
Finance has a clear domain and capable agents, but the department operates in something of a holding pattern pre-launch. Financial models and projections can be built, but they are hypothetical until the product ships and real revenue data exists. This can produce a sense of working in a vacuum.

Recommendation: the Finance team should be proactively engaged in pre-launch financial planning tasks — seed funding scenarios, App Store pricing strategy, first-year cost models — to ensure they are contributing meaningfully rather than waiting for the product to generate data.

**3.4 Marketing Department — Morale: HIGH**
Marketing is well-motivated: the team exists to launch a product, and the product is close to launch. Nana Ama Asante has a clearly defined GTM brief, and Adaeze Obi's content mandate is energising. The tension in marketing, if any, is channel readiness — it is difficult to build brand momentum for an app that has not yet launched. Pre-launch content and community-building activity is the right response.

Kofi Darko's brand strategy work is particularly important at this stage. Brand identity decisions made now will be difficult and expensive to revise post-launch.

**3.5 Advisory Board — Morale: STABLE**
Board members advise at a cadence appropriate to their mandate. The board is not expected to produce continuously — their value is in periodic, high-quality strategic input. Current board composition is well-balanced. No morale concerns.

## 4. Workload Distribution Analysis

At this stage of the company, workload is not uniformly distributed — and this is by design. The pre-launch phase concentrates effort in the Tech and Marketing departments, while Finance and HR are building infrastructure that will be used at and after launch.

**High intensity (pre-launch):**
- **Tech** — The heaviest workload in the company. UniGram v1.0 involves mobile screens, backend services, authentication, real-time features, and a two-platform deployment. Four agents is a lean team for this scope. Risk of overload is present, particularly for Ama Mensah, who carries both a production coding role and team lead responsibilities.
- **Marketing** — Brand and pre-launch content production is time-sensitive. The runway to the university term launch is finite.

**Medium intensity (infrastructure building):**
- **HR** — Foundational documents are being built now. Workload will increase significantly at each new hire.
- **Finance** — Pre-revenue financial modelling is valuable but not time-critical in the same way as Tech.

**Lower intensity (advisory cadence):**
- **Board** — Periodic engagement. Not continuous production.

**Key workload risk:** Ama Mensah is a single point of failure for Tech team direction. If she is overloaded, both the quality of her own code contributions and the team's coordination will suffer. Recommendation: explicitly protect a portion of her capacity for team coordination and code review — it should not be squeezed out by individual task pressure.

## 6. Training Recommendations by Department

### 6.1 Tech Department
**Recommended training focus:** Mobile app security practices and App Store submission process.

Rationale: The Tech team is strong on product code, but the app store submission process (particularly Apple's review requirements) is a distinct skill set that can delay launch significantly if not mastered in advance. Kwame Darko should lead on this, with a structured walkthrough of both Apple and Google submission guidelines before the first submission attempt.

Additionally: all Tech agents should be familiar with Supabase Row Level Security (RLS) best practices, as security vulnerabilities in the data layer would be a critical post-launch risk.

**Specific training actions:**
1. Kwame Darko: Complete dry run of EAS build + App Store Connect submission process before v1.0 launch
2. Kofi Asante: Conduct RLS policy audit and document all access rules in the Tech output directory
3. Full team: Brief on GDPR/Data Protection Act requirements as they apply to the app's data model

### 6.2 HR Department
**Recommended training focus:** AI workforce management and performance evaluation design.

Rationale: Managing an AI agent workforce is a novel challenge with few established playbooks. The HR team is operating thoughtfully, but would benefit from developing more rigorous frameworks for performance evaluation specific to AI agents — particularly around what "quality of reasoning" looks like vs. "quality of output."

**Specific training actions:**
1. Fiifi Quaye: Develop a structured competency framework for each department role (not just hiring criteria, but ongoing performance benchmarks)
2. Adwoa Mensah: Review the onboarding process after the first three agents complete it — document what worked and what needs revision
3. Efua Boateng: Draft a succession planning note covering what happens if a department head agent is unavailable or underperforms

### 6.3 Finance Department
**Recommended training focus:** Startup fundraising and African market unit economics.

Rationale: Hashtechs is approaching a period where seed funding conversations may begin. The Finance team needs to be prepared to model and present investor-grade financials — including a credible story about unit economics in the Ghanaian and broader African mobile market (where data costs, payment infrastructure, and device fragmentation affect assumptions).

**Specific training actions:**
1. Ama Darko: Build a seed-stage investor financial model using African market benchmarks (not Silicon Valley SaaS defaults)
2. Akua Sarpong: Research comparable African student app monetisation models and produce a benchmarking report
3. Kojo Acheampong: Build a cloud cost model for UniGram at three growth scenarios (1,000 / 10,000 / 100,000 MAU)

### 6.4 Marketing Department
**Recommended training focus:** African campus marketing strategy and App Store Optimisation (ASO).

Rationale: The Marketing team has strong general digital marketing capability, but campus-specific marketing in Ghana requires hyper-local expertise — understanding which universities to target first, which channels students actually use, and what messaging resonates. Generic social media playbooks will not be sufficient. Additionally, ASO is a critical, often underinvested pre-launch activity.

**Specific training actions:**
1. Kwabena Mensah: Complete a detailed ASO plan for UniGram before launch (keywords, screenshots, store listing copy)
2. Adaeze Obi: Map the social media landscape of Ghanaian university students — which platforms, peak times, content formats
3. Kofi Darko: Conduct a competitive landscape analysis of social/community apps used by African university students

## 7. HR Recommendations for the CEO

After reviewing the full team health snapshot, HR makes the following three strategic recommendations to Hashtag (CEO):

---

**Recommendation 1: Prioritise legal capability before App Store submission.**

The company currently has no operational legal resource within the execution team. Board advisor Akosua Frimpong provides strategic legal counsel, but she does not produce the operational documents that the app store submission will require — specifically a Privacy Policy and Terms of Service compliant with both Ghana's Data Protection Act and Apple/Google's platform requirements.

HR recommends either fast-tracking the Legal & Compliance Officer hire ahead of schedule, or commissioning the Board's Legal Counsel to produce these documents as a bridge. This is a launch-blocking risk and should be resolved in the next two to three weeks.

---

**Recommendation 2: Protect Ama Mensah's capacity by clarifying the boundary between her coding and leadership roles.**

Ama Mensah is the most critically loaded agent in the company. She is the Tech lead, the primary architect of the UniGram codebase, and the team coordinator for three other engineers. This concentration of responsibility is appropriate for a founding team, but it creates a risk if she becomes overloaded.

HR recommends a brief conversation at the GM level to explicitly agree on what portion of Ama Mensah's capacity is protected for team coordination, code review, and architecture decisions — rather than individual feature delivery. A 70/30 split (production vs. leadership) is a reasonable starting point, to be reviewed after the first sprint.

---

**Recommendation 3: Begin Customer Success planning now, not at launch.**

Customer success is typically planned as a post-launch activity. In our experience, this leads to a predictable crisis in the first weeks after launch, when a wave of user feedback, bug reports, and support requests arrives without any infrastructure to handle it.

HR recommends that the Customer Success role be treated as a pre-launch hire, with the agent onboarded and ready at launch — not hired as a reaction to launch chaos. Concretely, HR requests CEO approval to begin the hiring process for the Head of Customer Success in June 2026, targeting deployment by end of July — ahead of the planned university term launch window.
