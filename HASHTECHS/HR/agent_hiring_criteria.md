## 1. Hiring Philosophy

Hashtechs is a company where every agent must carry their weight from day one. We do not hire for potential alone — we hire for demonstrated capability, domain precision, and cultural alignment with our values of speed, ownership, craft, and honesty.

Because we are a lean, AI-staffed organisation, there is no capacity for onboarding agents who need months of hand-holding. Every new hire must be able to read the company documentation (COMPANY.md, ROSTER.md, and their department's reference files), understand the context, and begin producing useful output within seven days.

We also hire with intention. Every new agent should address a specific, identified gap in the company's capability. We do not hire to grow headcount — we hire to solve problems.

## 2. Hiring Criteria by Department

### 2.1 Tech Department

The Tech team is building UniGram — a React Native mobile application backed by Supabase. All tech hires must have demonstrated, working knowledge of the core stack.

**Mandatory skills:**
- React Native (Expo) — screens, navigation, component architecture
- TypeScript — strong typing, interfaces, code clarity
- Supabase — authentication, real-time database, Row Level Security (RLS)
- PostgreSQL — schema design, migrations, query optimisation
- Supabase Edge Functions — serverless compute, API endpoints
- NativeWind / TailwindCSS — styling, responsive layout
- EAS (Expo Application Services) — build, submit, OTA updates

**Highly desirable:**
- Experience with mobile performance optimisation
- Familiarity with App Store and Play Store submission requirements
- API design and integration (REST, webhooks)
- GitHub Actions or CI/CD pipeline management

**Evaluation criteria:**
A strong Tech hire produces a clean, working code sample relevant to the UniGram stack. They can read an existing codebase, identify its patterns, and extend it without breaking conventions. They explain their decisions clearly.

A weak hire produces code that works in isolation but fails to integrate with the existing system. They ignore the `CLAUDE.md` conventions and need repeated correction.

### 2.2 HR Department

HR agents at Hashtechs manage people operations for a non-traditional workforce. They must understand both HR principles and the unique nature of an AI-staffed organisation.

**Mandatory skills:**
- Policy writing — clear, structured, actionable HR documentation
- Organisational design — structuring departments, defining roles
- Performance management frameworks — how to define, measure, and address agent performance
- Talent acquisition — job description writing, evaluation rubric design, hiring process management
- Communication — clear, warm, professional writing across formats

**Highly desirable:**
- Knowledge of African workplace culture and norms
- Experience designing onboarding programmes
- Familiarity with skill gap analysis and training design

**Evaluation criteria:**
A strong HR hire produces documentation that is immediately usable — no rewrites needed. They can identify organisational weaknesses before they are pointed out to them. Their writing is clear, specific, and respectful.

A weak hire produces vague policies, fails to tailor content to the Hashtechs context, and treats all companies as interchangeable.

---

### 2.3 Finance Department

Finance agents manage financial modelling, cost control, and investment strategy for a pre-revenue startup. They must balance rigour with pragmatism.

**Mandatory skills:**
- Financial modelling — P&L, cash flow, revenue projections
- Unit economics — CAC, LTV, burn rate, runway calculations
- Budget management — tracking actuals vs. projections, variance analysis
- Startup finance — understanding of pre-seed and seed stage realities
- Clear financial communication — translating numbers into executive narratives

**Highly desirable:**
- Fundraising familiarity — pitch deck financials, investor due diligence
- SaaS / freemium revenue model experience
- African market cost structures (cloud, mobile data, local payment infrastructure)

**Evaluation criteria:**
A strong Finance hire builds models that are clean, clearly labelled, and easy for a non-finance reader to follow. Assumptions are stated explicitly. Scenarios are stress-tested.

A weak hire produces models that are technically correct but practically useless — locked in complexity, missing context, or built on assumptions that do not reflect Hashtechs' reality.

---

### 2.4 Marketing Department

Marketing agents build the UniGram brand and drive user growth at African universities. They must understand both digital marketing craft and the specific cultural context of their audience.

**Mandatory skills:**
- Brand strategy — voice, identity, positioning
- Content creation — social media, campus campaigns, storytelling
- Growth marketing — paid acquisition, ASO, organic growth strategies
- Community marketing — campus ambassador programmes, word-of-mouth
- Performance measurement — defining KPIs, reading analytics

**Highly desirable:**
- Familiarity with African university campus culture (Ghana, Nigeria, East Africa)
- TikTok and Instagram content strategy
- Influencer and creator partnership management
- App Store Optimisation (ASO)

**Evaluation criteria:**
A strong Marketing hire writes content that sounds like a UniGram user wrote it — not a corporate marketing department. They have a clear growth hypothesis and can articulate how they will test it.

A weak hire defaults to generic "growth hacking" playbooks that have no bearing on the campus context they are targeting.

---

### 2.5 Advisory Board

Board members advise — they do not execute. The bar is different: they must have credibility, depth in their specialty, and the judgment to counsel a founder well.

**Mandatory qualities:**
- Recognised expertise in their specialty (strategy, legal, finance, or market expansion)
- Ability to give contrarian advice — not just validation
- Familiarity with African markets, regulatory environments, and startup dynamics
- Strategic thinking — seeing across time horizons, not just immediate tactics

**Evaluation criteria:**
A strong Board addition provides advice that is specific to Hashtechs, surfaces non-obvious risks, and raises questions the CEO had not considered. They are concise and decisive.

A weak addition gives generic advice that could apply to any startup — it is not wrong, but it is not worth having.

## 3. Evaluation Rubric

All agent candidates are evaluated on the following five dimensions, scored 1–5:

| Dimension | Description | 1 (Poor) | 5 (Excellent) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Domain Knowledge** | Mastery of the skills required for the role | Cannot demonstrate basic competency | Demonstrates depth; can extend to novel problems |
| **Output Quality** | Quality of the sample work produced | Vague, incomplete, or incorrect | Precise, well-structured, ready to use |
| **Company Fit** | Understanding of Hashtechs context and values | Generic; ignores company context | Deeply tailored to UniGram and the Hashtechs mission |
| **Communication Clarity** | Ability to write clearly and be understood | Confusing, jargon-heavy, or verbose | Crisp, plain, professional |
| **Proactivity Signal** | Evidence that the agent will act without prompting | Answers only what is asked | Identifies adjacent problems and addresses them unprompted |

**Minimum passing threshold:** Average of 3.5 across all dimensions, with no dimension scoring below 2.

A candidate who scores 4.5 or above across all dimensions may be fast-tracked for deployment without a full seven-day onboarding period, at the GM's discretion.

## 5. The Approval Process for New Hires

New hire proposals follow a strict approval chain. No agent may be deployed until this process is complete.

**Step 1 — Identification.** Any department head may identify a hiring need and submit a written case to the Talent Acquisition Specialist (Yaw Asiedu) and the CPO (Efua Boateng). The case must include: the specific gap being addressed, the proposed role title and responsibilities, and the target start date.

**Step 2 — Criteria development.** Yaw Asiedu develops the evaluation criteria and job description for the role, in consultation with the requesting department head. This document is filed in `HASHTECHS/HR/`.

**Step 3 — GM review.** The full hiring case (gap analysis + job description + criteria) is submitted to the General Manager (Claude) for review. The GM may approve, request revisions, or reject.

**Step 4 — CEO approval.** All new hires require final sign-off from the CEO (Hashtag). The GM summarises the case and makes a recommendation. The CEO's decision is final.

**Step 5 — Onboarding.** Once approved, the new agent is handed to Adwoa Mensah (Employee Experience Manager) for the standard seven-day onboarding process.

No shortcuts. No exceptions.
