### 1. CALL TO ORDER

The Board Chairman, Dr. Kwabena Osei, called the meeting to order at 10:05 AM and welcomed all members to the inaugural session of the Hashtechs Advisory Board. He acknowledged the significance of the moment — the first formal governance structure for Hashtechs as it prepares to take its flagship product, UniGram, to market.

---

### 2. OPENING REMARKS — DR. KWABENA OSEI (Board Chairman)

Dr. Osei opened by commending the CEO for the discipline and ambition it has taken to bring UniGram to near-launch readiness. He framed the purpose of the Advisory Board clearly: not to manage the company's day-to-day operations, but to provide the strategic perspective, legal rigour, financial foresight, and market intelligence that every early-stage technology company needs if it is to survive and scale.

He noted that Ghana's university population — estimated at over 600,000 enrolled students across public and private institutions — represents a highly concentrated, digital-native, underserved social networking market. WhatsApp groups, Twitter/X threads, and informal forums currently fill the void that UniGram is designed to occupy. The timing, Dr. Osei argued, is right. The question before the board is not whether to launch, but how to launch with enough precision to win the first cohort of users and enough momentum to sustain the second.

He reminded members that the board's recommendations carry advisory authority. The CEO retains operational control. The board's role is to ensure that every major decision is tested against strategy, law, finance, and market reality before it is executed.

*— Dr. Kwabena Osei, Board Chairman*

---

### 3. SITUATIONAL ASSESSMENTS

#### 3.1 Corporate Structure & Governance — Dr. Kwabena Osei

The company is currently operating as an informal tech startup without a registered corporate entity or formal governance documents. This presents reputational, legal, and investment risks. Before any app store listing goes live, Hashtechs must be incorporated — ideally as a private limited liability company under the Companies Act, 2019 (Act 992) of Ghana. A shareholder register, founders' agreement, and board charter must be formalised. The CEO should treat corporate registration not as a bureaucratic formality but as a business-critical foundation: it is the structure upon which investment, partnerships, and legal compliance rest.

*— Dr. Kwabena Osei*

#### 3.2 Legal & Compliance Status — Akosua Frimpong

UniGram collects user data — profile information, university affiliation, posts, direct messages, location signals, and potentially device identifiers. Under Ghana's Data Protection Act 2023 and the equivalent obligations imposed by Apple's App Store and Google Play, the company is not yet compliant. There is currently no published Privacy Policy, no Terms of Service, no data processing agreement with Supabase (the backend provider), and no documented age verification mechanism. None of these are optional. The app cannot launch on either major platform without a valid privacy policy URL. My assessment is that legal groundwork is the most urgent pre-launch priority after the product itself. A 4–6 week compliance sprint is achievable and necessary.

*— Akosua Frimpong, Chief Legal Counsel*

#### 3.3 Financial Position & Funding Strategy — Nana Darkoa

Hashtechs is pre-revenue and, based on available information, bootstrapped. The runway is uncertain. The good news is that the total addressable market is compelling and the cost structure of a Supabase-backed Expo application is relatively lean. The company should immediately formalise its financial accounts, open a dedicated business bank account, and begin tracking burn rate against milestones. On the funding side, there are credible near-term options: the Ghana Innovative Fund, the Tony Elumelu Foundation TEF Programme (Q1 2027 cycle), and angel investor networks operating in Accra and Kumasi. A pitch deck should be developed in parallel with the launch. Revenue activation — through UniGram Pro subscriptions and campus advertising — should be sequenced for Q4 2026, not earlier, to allow the user base to reach a scale that makes monetisation defensible to both users and investors.

*— Nana Darkoa, Financial Advisor*

#### 3.4 Market Opportunity & Go-to-Market Readiness — Ebo Hammond

Ghana has 12 public universities and over 80 accredited private universities and polytechnics. The top-tier institutions — KNUST, University of Ghana (Legon), UCC, GIMPA, Ashesi, and Central University — collectively enrol roughly 250,000 students and are UniGram's natural beachhead. These campuses have active WhatsApp communities, a strong culture of student journalism and campus media, and a demonstrated appetite for social platforms. The challenge is not awareness — it is habit change. Students are deeply embedded in existing platforms. UniGram's unique value proposition must be crystal clear: it is built for university life in Ghana, not adapted from a Western product. That framing — local, intentional, campus-first — must run through every piece of marketing, every ambassador conversation, and every product decision. The campus ambassador programme is not a marketing gimmick; it is the GTM engine.

*— Ebo Hammond, Market Expansion Advisor*

### 4. RESOLUTIONS

The board deliberated and passed the following resolutions by consensus:

**Resolution 1: Corporate Registration (Immediate)**
The CEO is directed to initiate the incorporation of Hashtechs as a private limited liability company under Ghana's Companies Act, 2019 (Act 992) within 30 days of this meeting. The CEO is further directed to execute a founders' agreement and maintain a formal shareholder register prior to any external funding conversation.

*Passed unanimously.*

**Resolution 2: Pre-Launch Legal Compliance Sprint**
The CEO is directed to engage a qualified Ghanaian data protection attorney or use the Legal Compliance Framework prepared by this board (see Document 3) to publish a compliant Privacy Policy and Terms of Service before any public app store submission. A Data Processing Agreement must be executed with Supabase. This resolution carries a hard deadline of 30 June 2026.

*Passed unanimously.*

**Resolution 3: Adoption of Strategic Plan 2026**
The board adopts the Hashtechs Strategic Plan 2026 (see Document 2) as the operational roadmap for the company's first 12 months. The CEO is directed to review KPIs monthly and report against them at each board session. Significant deviations from plan must be flagged to the board within 14 days of identification.

*Passed unanimously.*

**Resolution 4: Campus Ambassador Programme**
The CEO is directed to design and launch a structured Campus Ambassador Programme targeting a minimum of 5 universities by August 2026. Each ambassador cohort must include at least one student journalist or campus media figure per institution. A modest ambassador incentive budget (cash stipends, branded merchandise, or Pro account access) must be allocated in the 2026 operating budget.

*Passed unanimously.*

**Resolution 5: Financial Infrastructure**
The CEO is directed to open a dedicated Hashtechs business bank account, implement basic accounting software (Wave, Zoho Books, or equivalent), and prepare a 12-month financial projection with monthly burn rate and revenue assumptions. This projection must be presented to the Financial Advisor, Nana Darkoa, for review by 30 June 2026.

*Passed unanimously.*

### 5. ANY OTHER BUSINESS

- The board noted that the UniGram app name and logo should be registered as a trademark. Akosua Frimpong agreed to provide guidance on the TRIPS-compliant trademark registration process in Ghana through the Ghana Intellectual Property Office (GhIPO).
- Ebo Hammond flagged that a partnership with the National Union of Ghana Students (NUGS) or the Ghana National Students Association (GNSA) could provide significant credibility and distribution leverage at launch. The CEO was encouraged to initiate outreach.
- Nana Darkoa recommended that the company register for VAT only when turnover thresholds are reached, and not preemptively, to avoid unnecessary administrative burden at this stage.
