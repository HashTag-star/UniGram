## CFO's Foreword

This roadmap is the Finance Department's central planning document for the 2026 financial year. It combines our revenue projections with our cost structures into a single month-by-month cash flow view, identifies the key financial milestones Hashtag and the leadership team should track, names the risks that could undermine our trajectory, and closes with three direct CFO recommendations for the launch phase.

## 3. Financial Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Cedi depreciation against USD | High | Medium | USD costs (Supabase, EAS, Apple) are fixed in USD; lock in annual payments where possible to reduce per-month FX exposure |
| Advertiser acquisition slower than modelled | Medium | High | Begin pre-launch sales outreach in Month 0; have 2 signed advertisers before launch day |
| Supabase Free tier limit hit unexpectedly | Medium | Medium | Monitor database size and auth usage weekly from M1; set upgrade trigger at 70% of Free tier limits |
| Low Pro subscription conversion (<3%) | Medium | Medium | Pro features must be genuinely valuable at launch — ensure at least 3 compelling premium features ship on Day 1 |
| User growth stalls after initial campus | High | High | Plan university 2 expansion no later than Month 3; growth is the most critical variable in all four revenue streams |
| Apple/Google app store rejection | Low | High | Submit for review 3 weeks before target launch date; have contingency TestFlight distribution for iOS |
| Team burnout / key person dependency | Medium | High | Document all processes; ensure at least 2 people understand each critical system (database, build pipeline) |
| Seed round investor drought | Low | Medium | Organic revenue model means Hashtechs does not depend on raising seed — it accelerates with it but survives without it |

## 4. CEO Financial Dashboard — 5 Weekly Metrics for Hashtag

These are the five numbers Hashtag should review every Monday morning. They are the leading and lagging indicators of financial health.

### Metric 1: Weekly Active Users (WAU)
**Why:** Revenue in all four streams is a direct function of user count. If WAU is not growing week-on-week, revenue growth will stall within 30–60 days. This is the earliest warning signal.
**Target trajectory:** 10%+ week-on-week growth in Months 1–3; 5–8% WoW growth in Months 4–8.

### Metric 2: Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) Projection
**Why:** MRR = (active Pro subscribers × GH₵ 15) + (active advertisers × GH₵ 500). It is the most predictable slice of revenue. Tracking it weekly against the monthly target tells you whether you are on track before the month closes.
**Target M6 MRR:** GH₵ 10,625 (Pro + Ads combined recurring component)

### Metric 3: Cash Runway (Months)
**Why:** Cash in bank ÷ monthly burn rate. At all times, Hashtag must know how many months of operation are funded without new revenue. If runway drops below 3 months, it is a crisis signal — begin emergency cost cuts or accelerate fundraising.
**Target:** Maintain minimum 4 months runway at all times.

### Metric 4: Advertiser Renewal Rate
**Why:** Campus advertisers pay GH₵ 500/month. A renewal (an advertiser paying for Month 2 and beyond) is proof that the advertising product delivers value. A renewal rate below 70% signals a product quality or reporting problem that must be fixed urgently — advertisers who churn tell others.
**Target:** 80%+ month-on-month renewal from Month 3.

### Metric 5: Cost Per Acquired User (CPAU)
**Why:** Total marketing spend ÷ new users acquired that month. This is the efficiency metric. If organic viral growth is working, CPAU should be under GH₵ 2. If it creeps above GH₵ 8, paid marketing channels are underperforming and strategy needs adjustment.
**Target:** Under GH₵ 3 in bootstrap phase; under GH₵ 5 in growth phase.

## 5. Three CFO Recommendations to the CEO — Launch Phase

### Recommendation 1: Sign Two Advertisers Before Launch Day

The single greatest financial risk in Month 1 is opening with zero revenue while absorbing launch costs (Apple account, Google Play, data, marketing). Two signed campus advertisers (GH₵ 1,000/month total) effectively cover all Month 1 infrastructure costs. They also give you a proof point and a story for users — "local businesses are already here." Begin advertiser conversations 3–4 weeks before launch. Offer a free first month to the first two advertisers if needed; the cost (GH₵ 1,000 in foregone revenue) is worth the momentum.

### Recommendation 2: Do Not Upgrade Infrastructure Until You Must — Then Move Fast

There is a temptation to upgrade to paid tiers "just in case." Resist it in the first two months — the Free tiers of Supabase and EAS are genuinely capable for early usage levels. However, once the upgrade trigger is hit (defined as 70% of Free tier limits in any metric), move within 48 hours, not 2 weeks. The worst outcome is a platform outage caused by hitting a Free tier ceiling mid-growth. Set automated monitoring alerts in Supabase dashboard from Day 1.

### Recommendation 3: Treat the Angel Round as a Milestone Race, Not a Pitch Race

Many founders begin fundraising before their numbers are ready. In Ghana's angel market, where investors know each other and referrals matter, a premature pitch that ends in a "not yet" can close doors for 12 months. The recommendation: do not make formal angel approaches until Month 4 milestones are met (defined in the funding strategy document). Use Months 1–3 to build relationships informally — attend MEST Africa events, engage the Ghana Angel Investor Network community, share the product with potential investors casually. When the numbers are ready, the relationship is already warm. A warm approach to a prepared investor closes in weeks, not months.
