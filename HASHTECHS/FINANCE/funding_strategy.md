## Executive Summary

Hashtechs is pursuing a capital-efficient funding strategy calibrated to the realities of Ghana's startup ecosystem. We are not chasing Silicon Valley-scale rounds. We are building deliberately, proving traction at every step, and raising only what is needed to reach the next inflection point. This document outlines the three-phase funding approach: Bootstrap (M1–M3), Angel Round (M4–M6), and Seed Round (M8–M12), alongside a concise investor pitch narrative, the Ghanaian funding landscape, use-of-funds breakdown, and the specific milestones investors will want to see before committing capital.

---

## Phase 1: Bootstrap (Month 1 – Month 3)

**Objective:** Prove the product works. Acquire first 1,000 users. Earn first revenue.

During the bootstrap phase, Hashtechs operates entirely on personal and founding team resources. No external capital is sought. The strategy is simple: minimise cash expenditure, maximise learning, and establish early revenue proof-points that strengthen the narrative for angel investors.

**Key financial targets for bootstrap phase:**
- Total cash needed: GH₵ 5,000–8,000 (covering Google Play one-time fee, Apple Developer account, first two months of domain/hosting, and data costs)
- Monthly operating cost: under GH₵ 2,000 (staying on Supabase Free + EAS Free)
- Revenue target by M3: GH₵ 4,635 (per revenue model projections)
- Milestone: Platform live, paying advertisers signed, first Pro subscribers converted

**Bootstrap principles:**
1. Use free tiers of every infrastructure tool until usage demands an upgrade
2. All marketing via organic channels: WhatsApp, Instagram, student WhatsApp groups
3. CEO (Hashtag) and core team work on deferred/equity compensation only
4. No office rent — remote-first operation
5. Every cedi spent must have a direct and traceable impact on user acquisition or product stability

## Phase 2: Angel Round (Month 4 – Month 6)

**Target raise:** $50,000 – $150,000 USD (GH₵ 750,000 – GH₵ 2,250,000)
**Structure:** SAFE note or convertible equity at a GH₵ / USD denomination, with a valuation cap of $600K–$1M
**Target close:** Month 5

By Month 4, Hashtechs should have demonstrable traction: paying users, active advertisers, and a clear growth curve. This is the story angel investors need. The angel round is not for building the product — the product is already built. It is for accelerating distribution and buying runway to hit Seed round milestones.

**Target angel investor profiles:**
- Ghanaian entrepreneurs who have exited or scaled a tech business (retail, fintech, logistics)
- Ghanaian diaspora in the UK, Canada, or USA with investment appetite for home-market plays
- University professors or administrators who understand the student market personally
- Angel networks with an Africa mandate

**Angel round approach:**
- Warm introductions through university networks, KNUST Innovation Hub, and MEST Africa community
- Pitch at Ghana Tech Lab events and Accra startup meetups
- Leverage any fintech/consumer tech communities in Kumasi and Accra
- Personal credibility of Hashtag (CEO) is a key trust signal — lean into the founder story

## Phase 3: Seed Round (Month 8 – Month 12)

**Target raise:** $300,000 – $500,000 USD (GH₵ 4,500,000 – GH₵ 7,500,000)
**Structure:** Priced equity round, 15–20% dilution, valuation implied at $1.5M–$2.5M pre-money
**Target close:** Month 10–11

The seed round is predicated on having achieved clear, investor-grade milestones by Month 8 (see milestones section below). At this stage, Hashtechs is no longer pitching potential — it is pitching proof. The seed round funds the expansion from 1–2 universities to 8–12 universities across Ghana, full product team hiring, and the beginning of Series A preparation.

**Target seed investors:**
- MEST Africa (pan-African accelerator + investor based in Accra)
- Injaro Investments (Ghana/West Africa-focused private equity and growth fund)
- Oui Capital (Lagos-based but increasingly active in Ghana)
- Greenhouse Capital (Nairobi/pan-Africa, consumer internet focus)
- Novastar Ventures (East/West Africa consumer)
- Tech Africa Angels network

## Investor Pitch Summary

### Problem
Ghana's 300,000+ university students lack a dedicated digital space that reflects their campus reality. General social platforms (Twitter, TikTok, Instagram) are not built for campus community — they offer no course-specific groups, no campus marketplace, no university events calendar, and no safe space for student-to-student connection. Students are fragmented across dozens of WhatsApp groups that are noisy, unstructured, and unmonetisable.

### Solution
UniGram is the university social network built for Ghanaian students. It combines social feeds, a campus marketplace, student community groups, campus events, and a Pro tier — all structured around university identity. Students sign up with their university email, verified to their institution. Everything on UniGram is relevant to their world.

### Market Size
- **TAM:** 300,000+ university students in Ghana; 4 million+ across West Africa
- **SAM (Year 1):** 50,000 students across 4–5 Ghanaian universities
- **SOM (Year 1 target):** 15,000 MAU (30% of SAM)
- Adjacent market: campus advertisers, local SMEs targeting students, university administrations

### Traction (to present at angel round — Month 4/5)
- Live on [X] universities
- [X] monthly active users
- [X] paying Pro subscribers
- [X] paying campus advertisers
- GH₵ [X] monthly revenue run rate
- Month-on-month user growth: [X]%

### The Ask (Angel Round)
$50,000 – $150,000 USD via SAFE note. 18-month runway. Used to reach 10,000 MAU and GH₵ 15,000/month revenue — the Seed round threshold.

## Ghana Funding Landscape

### Key Players

| Organisation        | Type                   | Focus                             | Notes                               |
|---------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| MEST Africa         | Accelerator + Fund     | Pan-African tech, based in Accra  | Portfolio includes Meqasa, Lori     |
| Injaro Investments  | Growth equity fund     | Ghana/West Africa SME & tech      | Patient capital, long-term view     |
| Ghana Angel Investor Network | Angel network | Early-stage Ghana startups       | Warm introductions recommended      |
| Ghana Venture Capital Trust Fund (VCTF) | Government fund | Ghanaian SMEs               | Slower process, non-dilutive grants available |
| Tony Elumelu Foundation | Grant + mentorship | African entrepreneurs          | $5K non-dilutive grant, annually    |
| Vodafone Ghana + MTN Ghana | Corporate VC | Student/mobile-adjacent plays    | Strategic fit for UniGram           |
| Accra-based Angels  | Individual HNWIs       | Diaspora and local tech investors | Access via startup events           |

### Landscape Notes
- Ghana's formal VC market is small relative to Nigeria or Kenya, but growing. The 2023–2025 wave of Ghanaian fintech and agritech exits has created a new class of angel investors with capital to deploy.
- Diaspora capital is significant — Ghanaian communities in London, Toronto, and Washington D.C. actively invest in home-market startups. Diaspora angels often have lower return hurdles and higher founder trust.
- Non-dilutive options (VCTF grants, Tony Elumelu Foundation) should be pursued in parallel — they cost equity and require time, but free capital is always superior.
- University partnerships (KNUST, UG, UCC) may offer in-kind support (office space, server co-location, access to student bodies) that reduces cash needs in the bootstrap and angel phases.

## Key Milestones Investors Want to See

### Before Angel Round (Month 4–5)
- [ ] Product live in app stores (iOS + Android) — no TestFlight-only distribution
- [ ] Minimum 1,500 registered users, 500+ MAU
- [ ] At least 3 paying campus advertisers with one renewal/extension
- [ ] At least 20 active Pro subscribers
- [ ] Month-on-month user growth of at least 50% for 2 consecutive months
- [ ] Clean cap table, company properly registered (Ghana Registrar-General)
- [ ] Basic privacy policy, terms of service, and data protection compliance (Ghana Data Protection Commission registration)

### Before Seed Round (Month 8–10)
- [ ] 8,000+ MAU across 3+ universities
- [ ] GH₵ 20,000+ monthly recurring revenue
- [ ] Clear unit economics: cost per acquired user under GH₵ 5
- [ ] Marketplace live with at least GH₵ 40,000 in monthly GMV
- [ ] 15+ active campus advertisers
- [ ] Demonstrated retention: 60-day user retention rate above 35%
- [ ] Angel round funds deployed responsibly, runway visible to Series A
- [ ] Named, experienced team (at minimum: CEO, CTO, Head of Growth)

## Strategic Notes for Hashtag (CEO)

1. **Raise on milestones, not projections.** Ghanaian angel investors are sophisticated — they have heard projections before. Every conversation goes better when you are showing real numbers, not models.
2. **The university network is your warm intro machine.** KNUST, UG, and UCC alumni are spread across Ghana's business community. Leverage them aggressively for investor introductions.
3. **Consider a SAFE note.** Simple Agreement for Future Equity avoids complex valuation negotiations at the angel stage and speeds up the close. Widely understood by Ghanaian investors who have dealt with international deal structures.
4. **Transparency builds trust.** Provide monthly investor updates even before you close a round. Build the relationship before the ask.
