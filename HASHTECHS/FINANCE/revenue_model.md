## Executive Summary

UniGram is a university social network targeting Ghana's 300,000+ university student population. This document outlines the four core revenue streams, pricing structures, monthly projections across a 12-month horizon, total Year 1 revenue forecasts, and a break-even analysis. All figures are denominated in Ghana Cedis (GH₵) with USD equivalents at an assumed exchange rate of GH₵ 15 = $1 USD.

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. UniGram Pro Subscription
**Price:** GH₵ 15/month (~$1 USD/month)

UniGram Pro offers premium features to students, including enhanced profile visibility, priority content in feeds, extended post reach, exclusive campus stickers/badges, and ad-free browsing. At a price point of GH₵ 15/month, this is deliberately accessible — roughly equivalent to a single meal at a campus canteen — reducing conversion friction significantly. Target conversion rate from MAU to Pro: 3–5% in early months, scaling to 6–8% as features mature.

### 2. Campus Advertisements
**Price:** GH₵ 500/month per advertiser

Local businesses adjacent to university campuses — food vendors, fashion boutiques, bookshops, ride-hailing services, mobile money agents, and tutoring centres — represent a dense, motivated advertiser base. Campus ads appear as native banner or card placements within university-specific feeds. Initial target: 2 advertisers at launch, scaling to 30+ by Month 12 across multiple universities. These advertisers value the hyper-targeted student audience far more than generic social media buys.

### 3. Marketplace Transaction Fees
**Fee:** 2.5% per transaction

UniGram's in-app marketplace allows students to buy, sell, and rent items — textbooks, electronics, fashion, and services. Hashtechs earns a 2.5% commission on each completed transaction processed through the platform. This stream begins modestly (low transaction volumes) but scales aggressively as the marketplace gains trust and liquidity. Average assumed transaction value: GH₵ 80. Fee per transaction: GH₵ 2.

### 4. Sponsored Posts
**Price:** GH₵ 200 per post

Brands, student organizations, NGOs, and on-campus businesses can pay for sponsored posts that appear in student feeds. Sponsored posts are clearly labelled and targeted by university, course of study, or year group. At GH₵ 200 per post, this is an entry-level influencer-alternative for small local brands. Target: 2 sponsored posts per month at launch, growing to 40+ by Month 12.

## Key Assumptions & Risks

1. **Exchange rate:** GH₵ 15 = $1 USD assumed. Cedi depreciation could affect USD-denominated costs (Supabase, EAS) but benefits local revenue comparisons.
2. **Advertiser acquisition:** Month 1 assumes 2 advertisers already signed before launch. This requires pre-launch sales outreach.
3. **Marketplace trust curve:** Early GMV is conservative. Trust-building, escrow mechanisms, and dispute resolution are critical to unlocking this stream.
4. **Conversion rate:** Pro conversion at 5% MAU is achievable but requires a genuinely compelling premium feature set at launch.
5. **University expansion:** M6+ growth assumes entry into 2–3 universities beyond the initial campus. Delays here compress the M12 MAU target.
